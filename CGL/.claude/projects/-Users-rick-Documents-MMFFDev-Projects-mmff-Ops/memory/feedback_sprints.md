name: Sprints are work bundles, not time boxes
description: In mmff-Ops, sprints are arbitrary groupings of related work — not fixed-duration containers. Do not apply traditional Scrum capacity logic.
type: feedback
---

Sprints in mmff-Ops are **arbitrary bundles of work**, not fixed-duration time boxes. They could be two weeks or any length. They exist only to group related activities into coherent chunks.

**Why:** The project uses sprints as organisational containers, not as Scrum iterations with velocity targets. Story points measure effort but don't constrain what fits in a sprint.

**How to apply:**
- Do not recommend splitting work across sprints because point total "feels high"
- Do not calculate velocity (points/duration) or use it for planning
- Sprint close is triggered by work completion or scope boundary, not by calendar
- When recommending sprint assignments, group by logical dependency and theme, not by capacity
- Anti-patterns from traditional Scrum that assume fixed time boxes (e.g. "overcommitting", "sprint spillover") do not automatically apply here
